# Wordloom School Data Processing Agreement / Processor Terms Draft

Status: Draft for school DPO/legal/data protection review

Last reviewed: May 2026

Core caveat: This draft is not legal advice, not approved contractual terms, and not a substitute for school/DPO/legal review.

This document is an internal draft to support review of possible school data processing terms for Wordloom. It is intended to help schools, DPOs, legal advisers and data protection reviewers assess what written processor terms may need to say before any school uses Wordloom under agreed terms.

ICO guidance and UK data protection law may change, including changes linked to the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025. This draft must be reviewed against current law, current ICO guidance, the school's own requirements, the product setup in use, supplier arrangements, and any written commercial or pilot terms before use.

## 2. Parties and roles

For school-managed pupil data, the school is usually the controller. The school decides how Wordloom is used with pupils and is usually responsible for lawful basis, notices to pupils, parents and carers, local governance, staff permissions, pupil data requests and school-side data protection decisions.

For school-managed use of the Wordloom product, Wordloom is likely to act as processor, processing school-managed personal data as instructed by the school and subject to written agreement.

Wordloom may act as controller for enquiries, demos, support, service administration, business administration and other processing where Wordloom decides the relevant purposes and means. Those controller activities should be documented separately and are not intended to be governed only by these processor terms.

The exact controller, processor or other role position may depend on the written arrangements, product setup, school procurement route, support arrangement, feature use and data involved. School/DPO/legal review is required before relying on this role analysis.

## 3. Subject matter and duration of processing

The subject matter of the processing is the provision, support, maintenance and security of Wordloom for school-managed spelling and literacy activity.

The processing may include:

- setting up and managing school, staff and pupil access;
- creating, assigning and completing spelling activities;
- recording attempts, responses, scores, progress and analytics evidence;
- supporting teacher review, class administration and school reporting where supported by the product;
- supporting teacher-facing AI features where used;
- responding to support, operational and security requests;
- maintaining service reliability, school scoping and access controls where supported by the product.

The duration of processing should be set out in the written agreement with the school. Unless separately agreed, processing should continue only for the period needed to provide, maintain, secure and support the service, handle agreed retention/export/deletion processes, and meet applicable legal or operational needs.

Duration, retention and end-of-service handling are to be confirmed in a formal retention/deletion/export procedure and written school terms.

## 4. Nature and purpose of processing

The nature of processing may include collection, recording, storage, organisation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission to relevant service providers, restriction, export, deletion, archiving and other operations needed to provide the service where supported by the product.

The purposes of processing may include:

- providing school-managed spelling and literacy activities;
- managing staff, pupil, class, assignment and school access;
- enabling pupils to complete assigned practice or tests;
- showing progress, completion, accuracy and analytics evidence to authorised staff;
- supporting teacher preparation and review;
- maintaining school scoping, role-based access and security controls where supported by the product;
- responding to school support requests and operational issues;
- using teacher-facing AI support where the school uses those features;
- maintaining records needed to operate and support the service.

Wordloom should not use school-managed personal data for purposes outside the school's documented instructions except where required by law or where Wordloom is acting as controller for a separately identified activity.

## 5. Types of personal data

Types of personal data may include, where applicable:

- school details, school identifiers and setup information;
- staff names, staff email addresses, roles, permissions, access approvals and support details;
- pupil names or school-managed pupil identifiers;
- pupil usernames, class or group membership, assignment information and school-managed credential information;
- spelling tests, word lists, assigned activities, submitted responses, attempt counts, scores, completion information, progress summaries and analytics evidence;
- Spelling Bee results where that feature is used;
- context support such as short sentences, meanings or related cache records where supported by the product;
- teacher analytics chat records or teacher-facing AI prompt/context information where those features are used;
- support, enquiry and operational information provided by school staff;
- technical, browser, session, device or access information needed for product operation, security and troubleshooting.

Special category data should not be intentionally added unless a school has separately assessed and instructed that processing. Staff should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into prompts, free-text fields or support requests.

## 6. Categories of data subjects

Categories of data subjects may include:

- pupils using school-managed Wordloom access;
- teachers, school staff, trust staff and administrators;
- school contacts involved in setup, support, procurement or administration;
- parents, carers or other individuals if their details are included in support or school communications.

Wordloom is intended for school-managed pupil use rather than independent public account creation by children.

## 7. Controller responsibilities

The school, as controller where applicable, is usually responsible for:

- deciding whether and how Wordloom is used with pupils;
- identifying an appropriate lawful basis and meeting transparency duties;
- providing or approving privacy notices to pupils, parents and carers where needed;
- deciding what pupil, staff and school data is added to Wordloom;
- managing staff roles, permissions, access approvals and leaver processes;
- managing pupil credential issuing, reset and local handling expectations;
- ensuring staff are trained to use Wordloom appropriately;
- handling pupil, parent, carer and staff data protection requests in the first instance for school-managed use;
- assessing whether a DPIA or other local information governance review is required;
- reviewing supplier, AI, security, retention, export and deletion arrangements for its own setting;
- giving documented instructions to Wordloom where Wordloom acts as processor.

The school should avoid instructing or permitting use of Wordloom in ways that add unnecessary personal data, expose another school's data, bypass access controls, misuse accounts or conflict with school safeguarding and data protection requirements.

## 8. Processor responsibilities

Where Wordloom acts as processor for school-managed use, Wordloom should:

- process personal data only on documented school instructions, unless required by law;
- inform the school if Wordloom believes an instruction may conflict with applicable data protection law, where appropriate and permitted;
- ensure people authorised to process school-managed personal data are subject to confidentiality obligations;
- use appropriate technical and organisational measures for the processing;
- assist the school, where applicable and where supported by the product, with data subject rights requests;
- assist the school, where applicable, with security, DPIA and consultation duties;
- support the school in relation to personal data breach concerns affecting school-managed data;
- use sub-processors only as set out in written terms or with appropriate written authorisation;
- make reasonable information available to help demonstrate compliance with agreed processor obligations, subject to confidentiality, security and commercial constraints;
- handle return, deletion, retention or export at the end of service as set out in written terms, product capabilities, legal/operational needs and school instructions.

These processor responsibilities are draft terms for review and should be checked against the current UK GDPR, ICO guidance, the school's requirements and the product setup in use.

## 9. Documented instructions

The school's documented instructions may include:

- the written school agreement, pilot terms, service order or support arrangement;
- agreed product configuration and school setup;
- authorised school administrator actions inside the product;
- written support requests or instructions sent by authorised school contacts;
- agreed retention, export, deletion or offboarding instructions;
- any other saved written instruction accepted by Wordloom.

Wordloom should process school-managed personal data as instructed by the school, unless required by law. If Wordloom is required by law to process personal data outside the school's instructions, Wordloom should tell the school before processing where legally permitted.

Instructions should be clear, capable of being saved, and given by authorised school contacts.

## 10. Confidentiality

Wordloom should ensure that people authorised to process school-managed personal data are subject to suitable confidentiality obligations.

Operational or support access should be limited to people who need access to provide, maintain, secure or support the service. Any school-side support request may require Wordloom to review relevant school, account, technical, usage or support information so that the issue can be understood and resolved.

Schools should also maintain confidentiality expectations for their own staff, accounts, pupil credentials, exports and local records.

## 11. Security measures

Wordloom should use appropriate technical and organisational measures for the processing, taking account of the nature of the processing and the risks to pupils, staff and schools.

Current product evidence indicates that Wordloom is designed to use school-scoped data areas, role-based access controls, staff access approvals, active school context, server-side helpers and row-level security where supported by the product. Pupil access uses school-managed credentials and server-side authentication or validation. Teacher sign-in may use Supabase Auth and Google OAuth.

Security measures should be reviewed against the current product, deployment and supplier setup. No online service can remove every risk. Product controls, hosting arrangements, suppliers and school requirements may change over time.

Security audit or certification status is to be confirmed.

## 12. Sub-processors

Wordloom may use third-party service providers to provide, support, secure or deliver the service. Depending on the setup and written terms, these providers may act as processors, sub-processors or technical service providers.

Providers visible or currently identified from the policy/evidence files include:

- Supabase, which may be used for database, authentication and server-side functions;
- Google OAuth, which may be used for teacher sign-in where enabled;
- OpenAI API, which may be used for teacher-facing AI support where those features are used;
- jsDelivr or similar delivery services, which may be used for browser delivery of frontend libraries where loaded.

Wordloom should not appoint a sub-processor for school-managed personal data except as authorised in the written terms or otherwise agreed in writing. Where general written authorisation is used, Wordloom should provide notice of intended material changes and a reasonable opportunity for the school to raise concerns, subject to written agreement.

Wordloom should require relevant sub-processors to provide appropriate protections for school-managed personal data, taking account of the services they provide and the written terms in place.

Supplier DPAs, sub-processor status, provider terms, regions, data residency and transfer safeguards are to be confirmed.

## 13. International transfers / data location caveat

Hosting/provider region, production data location, supplier locations and transfer safeguards are to be confirmed.

This draft should not be read as a representation that school-managed data is stored or processed only in any particular country. Schools should review the provider setup, supplier terms, transfer safeguards and local procurement requirements before use.

Where an international transfer is involved, Wordloom should process personal data only as instructed by the school and subject to appropriate written terms and safeguards where required.

Production Supabase region/configuration/security terms, Google OAuth setup, OpenAI data-processing terms, AI data-flow details, email/support provider, payment provider if applicable, and monitoring/error logging provider if applicable are all to be confirmed.

## 14. Data subject rights assistance

For school-managed pupil accounts, pupils, parents and carers should usually contact the school in the first instance for pupil data requests. The school is usually best placed to verify the request and decide how it should be handled.

Where Wordloom acts as processor, Wordloom should assist the school, where applicable and where supported by the product, with requests relating to access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection or other data subject rights.

The form and timing of assistance should be set out in written terms or support arrangements. Wordloom should not respond directly to school-managed pupil data requests as controller unless that role position applies to the relevant activity or the school has instructed Wordloom to assist in a particular way.

## 15. DPIA / consultation assistance

Schools are responsible for deciding whether a DPIA is required, completing it, approving it through their own governance and reviewing it with the school or trust DPO where appropriate.

Where Wordloom acts as processor, Wordloom should assist the school, where applicable and taking account of the nature of processing and information available to Wordloom, with information reasonably needed for DPIAs, risk reviews and consultation duties.

Assistance may include product information about data types, purposes, access controls, school scoping, AI-supported features, sub-processors, retention/export/deletion notes and known limitations.

The DPIA support page is not a completed school DPIA and does not replace the school's own assessment.

## 16. Personal data breach support

Schools can contact Wordloom about security concerns, suspected unauthorised access, account issues or product behaviour that may affect school data or pupil use.

Where Wordloom acts as processor and becomes aware of a personal data breach concern affecting school-managed personal data, Wordloom should notify/support the school without undue delay after becoming aware, in accordance with written terms and applicable law.

Support may include relevant information reasonably available to Wordloom, such as the nature of the concern, categories of data or users affected where known, likely consequences where known, steps taken or proposed, and contact routes for follow-up.

The formal incident/breach process is to be confirmed.

Wordloom should not be treated as the school's safeguarding reporting route. Safeguarding concerns involving pupils should be handled through the school's own safeguarding procedures.

## 17. Deletion, return and retention at end of service

At the end of service, Wordloom should handle school-managed personal data in accordance with written terms, product setup, legal/operational needs and school instructions.

End-of-service handling may include return/export, deletion, archiving, restriction or retention for a limited operational or legal purpose, depending on the record type and agreed process.

Current product evidence indicates that analytics exports are available for some teacher views and that some records can be archived/restored or deleted by privileged users. These capabilities should not be treated as a full school offboarding or data subject request process without further review.

Formal retention/deletion/export procedure, full school offboarding coverage and supported export/deletion scope are to be confirmed.

## 18. Audit and information provision

Where Wordloom acts as processor, Wordloom should provide reasonable information needed to demonstrate compliance with agreed processor obligations, subject to confidentiality, security and commercial constraints.

The form of audit and information provision should be set out in written terms. It may include written responses, policy documents, supplier information, security summaries, DPIA support information, evidence summaries, or other reasonable review materials.

Any audit or inspection should be proportionate to the nature of the processing, the risks involved, school procurement requirements, confidentiality obligations, security needs and the resources reasonably available.

Security audit/certification status, supplier due diligence and independent assurance materials are to be confirmed.

## 19. School responsibilities and acceptable use

The school should:

- manage staff access, roles, approvals and leaver processes;
- ensure pupils use school-managed access appropriately;
- handle pupil credential issuing, reset and local confidentiality expectations;
- review AI-supported wording before assigning pupil-visible content;
- avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into AI prompts, free-text fields or support requests;
- use the minimum information needed for preparation, analytics and support;
- avoid uploading harmful, unlawful or inappropriate content;
- avoid trying to access another school's data or bypass controls;
- maintain local device, browser, filtering, monitoring and safeguarding arrangements;
- assess accessibility, inclusion, age and vulnerability considerations for its own pupils;
- assess retention, export, deletion and incident response expectations for its own setting.

Acceptable use wording should be aligned with separate written school terms and public terms before use.

## 20. AI-supported features

Wordloom may use AI in limited teacher-facing parts of the product to support spelling preparation and teacher review. AI may support preparation tasks such as suggesting short sentences, word meanings or spelling materials. AI may also support teacher-facing analytics summaries or questions where those features are used.

Pupils are not provided with open AI chat in the current policy/evidence files, and AI is not called while pupils complete spelling tests. Pupil spelling scores are not judged by AI; scoring is based on the submitted answer against the expected spelling or task response.

Teacher-facing AI prompts may include words, instructions, questions or analytics context needed to provide the requested support. Staff should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into AI prompts and should use the minimum information needed.

AI-supported context is designed to avoid giving spelling clues, but every possible clue or unsuitable output should not be assumed to be prevented. Teachers should review AI-supported wording before assigning work where it will be visible to pupils.

OpenAI data-processing terms, AI data-flow details, deployed `ai-suggest` source/provider, model/provider configuration and AI assurance notes are to be confirmed.

## 21. Support and operational access

Wordloom support or operational access should be limited to what is needed to provide, maintain, secure or support the service.

Support requests may require Wordloom to review relevant school, account, technical, usage or support information so that issues can be understood and resolved. Schools should avoid sending unnecessary personal data in support requests.

Support access procedures, support provider, email provider, access logging approach and operational review process are to be confirmed.

## 22. Contact and notices

For school data processing, supplier, DPIA, AI, support or security questions, schools may contact:

`hello@wordloom.co.uk`

For pupil data requests connected to school-managed use, pupils, parents and carers should usually contact the school in the first instance.

Written terms should confirm notice addresses, authorised school contacts, escalation routes, security reporting routes and any response-time commitments.

ICO fee/registration position is to be confirmed.
