Accessibility Statement

Last reviewed: May 2026

Wordloom is designed to support accessible use in schools. This statement explains accessibility-related features that may help pupils and staff, known limitations, and how schools can share feedback.

1. Purpose of this statement

This statement is for schools, staff, pupils, parents and carers who want to understand Wordloom's current accessibility approach.

It is not a formal accessibility audit or compliance certificate. Wordloom should be tested by schools in their own devices, browsers and assistive technology setup before relying on it for a particular access need.

2. Accessibility approach

Wordloom is a school-facing spelling app. The product is intended to keep pupil work focused, structured and teacher-managed, while continuing to improve accessibility across pupil and staff workflows.

Accessibility needs can vary by pupil, school device, browser, assistive technology and local settings. Wordloom aims to improve these areas over time and welcomes feedback about barriers schools encounter.

3. Features that may support accessibility

Some parts of Wordloom include accessibility-related controls or preferences where available. Pupil Reading help controls may support overlay colour, font, spacing and text size choices.

Accessibility preferences may be saved in browser storage so they can be reapplied during future use on the same browser. Some pupil spelling activities also include audio or listen support where browser speech synthesis and product settings allow it.

The public homepage and policy pages use semantic sections, labelled navigation, image alternative text and visible focus styles for links and buttons.

4. Known limitations

No formal WCAG 2.2 AA audit has yet been completed. Colour contrast, full keyboard coverage and screen-reader support should not be assumed across every part of the app without dedicated testing.

Browser speech and audio support depends on the device, browser, school settings and available voices. Complex teacher dashboards, analytics, charts, custom controls and game interactions should be tested with school devices and assistive technology.

Reduced motion support should not be assumed unless it is specifically confirmed for the relevant part of the product.

5. School testing and device context

Schools should test Wordloom on the devices, browsers, network settings and assistive technology that pupils and staff will actually use.

Where a pupil has a known access need, the school should decide whether Wordloom is suitable in that context and whether adjustments, alternative arrangements or staff support are needed.

6. Feedback and contact

Schools can contact Wordloom about accessibility barriers, support needs or parts of the product that are difficult to use.

Please include the device, browser, assistive technology if relevant, the page or workflow affected, and a short description of the problem. Schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.

7. Future improvements

Wordloom is continuing to improve accessibility. Future work may include clearer testing, better keyboard and screen-reader review, more consistent accessibility controls, and improved documentation for schools.

8. Updates to this statement

Wordloom may update this statement as the product, accessibility features, testing evidence or school feedback changes. Future wording should continue to reflect what has actually been tested or implemented.
