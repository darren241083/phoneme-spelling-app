Privacy Notice

Last reviewed: May 2026

This notice explains how Wordloom may process information where a school uses Wordloom for spelling tests, pupil practice, teacher analytics, support, demos, or business administration. It is public transparency information, not the full Data Processing Agreement.

1. Who this notice is for

This notice is for schools, staff, pupils, parents and carers who want to understand how Wordloom may handle information in and around the service.

Wordloom is a school-facing spelling app. Where a school uses Wordloom, pupil accounts are usually school-managed rather than accounts pupils set up independently.

2. Who controls pupil data

For school-managed pupil accounts, the school is usually the data controller. The school decides how Wordloom is used with its pupils and is usually responsible for the lawful basis, notices to families, and pupil data requests.

Wordloom is likely to act as a processor for school-managed pupil use, acting on the school's instructions. Product setup or customer arrangements may vary, so this notice should not be treated as legal certainty for every situation.

Wordloom may act as controller for website enquiries, demo or trial contact, support emails, and business administration.

3. What information Wordloom may process

Wordloom may process school and staff account data, such as school details, staff names, staff email addresses, access roles, permissions, support details, and setup information.

Where a school uses pupil accounts, Wordloom may process pupil account data, class or form membership, spelling tests and assignments, pupil spelling attempts, submitted responses, scores, progress evidence, and related analytics.

Wordloom may also process context support such as sentences, meanings, and school-scoped context cache records, plus teacher analytics chat or support information where those features are used.

4. Why the information is used

Information is used to provide the service, manage school and staff access, create and assign spelling work, let pupils complete assigned practice, show progress evidence, support teacher review, and respond to support or enquiry messages.

Wordloom aims to use the minimum information needed for these purposes and to keep school-managed data within school-scoped product areas where the product supports that.

5. How pupil spelling evidence is used

Pupil spelling evidence may include assigned words, submitted answers, whether an answer matched the expected spelling or task response, attempt counts, completion information, and progress summaries.

This evidence is used to help teachers review spelling activity and plan follow-up. Baseline spelling evidence remains separate from optional meaning or context support.

6. AI and analytics

Wordloom may use analytics to help teachers understand spelling patterns, progress, repeated attempts, and areas that may need further practice.

Teacher-facing AI support may be used for preparation or analytics questions. Prompts may include the words, instructions, questions, or analytics context needed to provide the requested support. Staff should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into AI prompts.

Pupils are not provided with open AI chat, and AI is not called while pupils are completing spelling tests.

7. Who can access information

Access is intended to be role-based. School staff may be able to access information depending on their role, school setup, and assigned permissions.

School-managed pupil data should be treated as school-scoped. Wordloom support or operational staff may access information where needed to provide, maintain, secure, or support the service.

8. Third-party services

Supabase may be used for database, authentication, and server-side functions. Google OAuth may be used for teacher sign-in. The OpenAI API may be used for teacher-facing AI support.

Other technical libraries or services may support delivery of the service. This notice is not a complete sub-processor list; a fuller sub-processors page can be provided separately.

9. How long information is kept

Records may be kept while needed to provide the service, support schools, maintain records, improve reliability, or meet operational or legal needs.

Retention and deletion may depend on the school's instructions, the product setup, the type of record, and operational requirements. Schools should contact Wordloom if they need to discuss data retention for their use of the service.

10. Data rights and requests

For school-managed pupil accounts, pupils, parents and carers should usually contact the school in the first instance for pupil data requests. The school is usually best placed to verify the request and decide how it should be handled.

For enquiries, demo or trial contact, support emails, or business administration data where Wordloom may act as controller, individuals can contact Wordloom directly.

11. Security and school scoping

Wordloom is designed to use school-scoped data areas and role-based access controls where the product supports those controls. These controls are intended to reduce access to information that is not needed for a user's role or school context.

No online service can remove every risk. Schools and staff should use appropriate account controls, avoid unnecessary personal data in prompts or free-text fields, and tell Wordloom about support or security concerns.

12. Contact

For questions about this notice, schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.

For pupil data requests connected to school-managed use, contact the school in the first instance.

13. Updates to this notice

Wordloom may update this notice as the product, school requirements, suppliers, or support processes change. Future wording should continue to describe what the product actually does and avoid unsupported compliance claims.
