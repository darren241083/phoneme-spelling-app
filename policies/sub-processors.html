Wordloom policy

Sub-processors

Last reviewed: May 2026

This page gives schools transparency about third-party services Wordloom may use to provide, support, or deliver the product.

1. Purpose of this page

This page helps schools understand the third-party services currently visible or known from the Wordloom product setup.

Schools should read this page alongside the Privacy Notice and School Data Use Statement.

2. How to read this list

The list is based on services currently visible or known from the product setup. It is not a formal legal schedule of every supplier or a substitute for any written agreement with a school.

Some providers may act as processors, sub-processors, or technical service providers depending on the school setup, product feature used, and written terms that apply.

From the visible repository and setup, the production hosting provider, hosting region or data residency position, email or support provider behind hello@wordloom.co.uk, payment provider, monitoring or error logging provider, formal supplier due diligence status, and exact deployed status of ai-suggest were not confirmed.

3. Current service providers

Supabase may be used for database, authentication, and server-side functions. This may include storing school-managed product data, handling teacher authentication sessions, and running supported server-side operations.

Google OAuth may be used for teacher sign-in where Google sign-in is used.

OpenAI API may be used for teacher-facing AI support where those features are used.

4. Technical libraries and delivery services

jsDelivr may be used for browser delivery of frontend library resources such as the Supabase browser client where loaded.

esm.sh may be used by Edge Functions for dependency imports.

Local SheetJS/xlsx vendored library runs locally in the browser for spreadsheet-related product features. No external service call was identified from the vendored file itself during this review.

These entries are included for transparency and should be read cautiously as technical delivery or library information, not as confirmation that every listed item receives school data.

5. AI-related providers

OpenAI API may support teacher-facing AI features, such as analytics support or content preparation support, where those features are used.

Teacher-facing prompts may include the words, instructions, questions, or analytics context needed to provide the requested support. Staff should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data and should use the minimum information needed.

Pupils are not provided with open AI chat, and AI is not called while pupils are completing spelling tests.

6. Authentication providers

Supabase Auth may be used to manage teacher authentication sessions and related sign-in behaviour.

Google OAuth may be used as the teacher sign-in route where Google sign-in is enabled or selected. Authentication behaviour may depend on the school's account setup and the product configuration in use.

7. Changes to providers

Wordloom may add, remove, or change providers as the product, school requirements, technical setup, or support arrangements change.

Where provider changes are relevant to school data processing, Wordloom may update this page or discuss the change with schools through the applicable school arrangements.

8. School questions and contact

Schools can contact Wordloom with supplier or school data processing questions, including questions about which providers are relevant to their setup.

For questions about this page, schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.

9. Updates to this page

Wordloom may update this page as the product, suppliers, technical delivery, school requirements, or support processes change.

Future wording should continue to describe services currently visible or currently understood, and avoid unsupported claims about supplier status, hosting, data residency, certifications, due diligence, or suitability for every school context.
