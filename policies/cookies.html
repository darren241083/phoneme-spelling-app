Cookie Notice

Last reviewed: May 2026

This notice explains how Wordloom may use cookies or similar browser storage for school-facing spelling activity, sign-in, sessions, preferences and product operation.

1. Purpose of this notice

This notice is for schools, staff, pupils, parents and carers who want to understand how Wordloom may use cookies or similar technologies in the website and app.

It is based on the product behaviour and files visible at the time of review. It should be updated if Wordloom adds new analytics, optional cookies or third-party tracking tools.

2. What cookies and similar technologies are

Cookies are small files that a website can ask a browser to store. Similar technologies include browser storage such as local storage, which can keep information on a device between visits.

These technologies may help a service remember a sign-in session, a selected school context, a user preference, or other information needed for the app to work as expected.

3. What Wordloom may use them for

Wordloom may use cookies or similar browser storage for login, security, session handling, school context, accessibility preferences and product operation.

For example, browser storage may help Wordloom remember whether a user chose the teacher or pupil route, keep a pupil session available after login, or preserve reading and display preferences.

4. Necessary product storage

Some storage may be necessary for Wordloom to provide core product features. Supabase authentication is configured to persist sign-in sessions in browser storage, with token refresh and sign-in redirect handling.

Wordloom may also use browser storage for pupil session details, active school context, accessibility settings and teacher draft state where those features are used.

Blocking necessary product storage may stop parts of Wordloom working, including sign-in, session continuity, school selection, accessibility preferences or pupil access.

5. Analytics, advertising and optional cookies

This notice does not currently describe advertising or marketing cookies because none were visible in the inspected public or app files.

Future analytics, advertising or optional cookies would require this notice to be updated and, where required, consent controls to be added before those technologies are used.

6. Managing cookies and browser storage

Users can control cookies and site storage through their browser settings. Browser controls usually allow users to block, delete or clear stored information for a site.

Schools may also manage browser and device settings through their own technical controls. Blocking or clearing storage may sign users out, reset preferences, or stop some Wordloom features working correctly.

7. Changes to this notice

Wordloom may update this notice as product features, authentication behaviour, browser storage use or third-party services change.

Future wording should continue to describe the technologies Wordloom actually uses and avoid unsupported claims about cookies, tracking or consent.

8. Contact

For questions about this notice, schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.
