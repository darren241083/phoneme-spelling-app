# Data Subject Request Handling Checklist

**Status:** Internal draft for review  
**Last reviewed:** 5 May 2026

## 1. Purpose

This checklist helps Wordloom record, triage and support requests relating to access, correction, deletion, restriction, export, objection and school-led instructions.

It is intended to support internal consistency, school/DPO review, evidence gathering, support workflows and careful handling of pupil, staff and school data requests. It should be used as a practical support document when gathering facts, checking relevant systems and recording authorised school instruction.

## 2. Status and limitations

This checklist is not a public policy page.

This checklist is not legal advice, is not a final legal data subject request procedure and does not claim that Wordloom is fully GDPR compliant. It supports request handling and evidence gathering, but it does not replace school/DPO/legal review.

The school/controller may need to decide how a request is handled. Wordloom may support the school/controller through technical checks, evidence gathering and manual support steps, where this is appropriate and authorised.

Legal grounds, deadlines, exemptions, refusal grounds and response wording should be confirmed through school/DPO/legal review. Unknowns should be marked clearly as "To be confirmed".

This checklist does not claim that all access, export, deletion or restriction requests can be completed instantly, fully automatically or without manual review.

## 3. Controller and support boundary

Schools are likely to act as controller for school and pupil use of Wordloom, but controller/processor positions must be confirmed in written terms and reviewed where needed.

Wordloom should not independently decide school-controlled requests unless this is clearly authorised and documented. Authorised school instructions should be recorded, including who gave the instruction, the date, the scope and any limits.

Requests received directly from pupils, parents/carers or staff may need referral to the school/controller where appropriate. Wordloom should avoid making firm legal claims or legal determinations when handling those requests.

## 7. Handling Notes By Request Type

### Access / Copy Request

- Support the school/controller in identifying relevant data areas.
- Confirm the request scope before export or disclosure.
- Avoid disclosing data about other pupils, staff or school users.
- Use manual review where data is mixed, contextual, derived or sensitive.

### Correction Request

- Confirm what record is disputed and the requested correction.
- Confirm authorised school instruction before changing school-controlled records.
- Record the before/after change where appropriate.
- Consider whether derived analytics, reports or related support records may be affected.

### Deletion / Erasure Request

- Confirm the scope, authority and relevant school/controller instruction.
- Review whether archive, restriction, deactivation or deletion is appropriate.
- Consider whether supplier logs, backups and support records have separate handling.
- Do not claim instant or complete deletion unless this has been verified.

### Restriction Request

- Record the requested restriction scope.
- Consider whether access should be limited, an account deactivated or processing paused.
- Confirm school/controller instruction before changing school-managed records.
- Record any manual support steps and open questions.

### Export Request

- Confirm the scope and requested format.
- Distinguish product exports, manual support exports and supplier-held records.
- Check for third-party data before sharing.
- Record any limitations, unresolved data areas or "To be confirmed" items.

### Objection / Concern

- Record the concern and the data area involved.
- Route to the school/controller/DPO/legal contact where relevant.
- Avoid making legal determinations without review.
- Record any evidence gathered and the agreed next step.

### AI Prompt / Output Concern

- Record the prompt/output concern and where it appeared.
- Check whether pupil or staff personal data may be involved.
- Review supplier/provider handling where relevant.
- Link to the AI risk assessment and incident process where needed.

## 9. Escalation Points

Situations requiring school/DPO/legal/controller review include:

- Unclear requester authority.
- Request from parent/carer/pupil/staff directly.
- Safeguarding concern.
- Incident or suspected breach.
- Request involving multiple pupils/staff.
- Deletion request affecting audit/security/learning records.
- Request involving AI prompts/outputs.
- Supplier or backup/log limitation.
- Disagreement about scope or response.
- Urgent or sensitive request.

## 10. Risk Notes

- Responding without confirming controller instruction.
- Disclosing data about another pupil or staff member.
- Assuming export or deletion is fully automated.
- Deleting records that may need review or retention.
- Overlooking derived analytics or AI/context support records.
- Missing supplier logs, backups or support records.
- Making unsupported public promises about deletion or export.
- Keeping an incomplete evidence trail.
- Relying on an unclear school contact or authorisation route.

## 12. Review Cadence

Review this checklist:

- Before first school rollout.
- When the data inventory changes.
- When export, deletion or offboarding processes change.
- When AI/data-flow behaviour changes.
- After a real request is handled.
- After an incident or near miss.
- After supplier changes.
- At least annually.
