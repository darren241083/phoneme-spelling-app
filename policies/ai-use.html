AI Use Statement

Last reviewed: May 2026

Wordloom uses AI in limited, teacher-facing parts of the product to support spelling preparation and teacher review. This statement explains what AI may support, what pupils do not do with AI, and the limits of that support.

1. Summary

Wordloom is designed around teacher-controlled spelling work for schools. AI may support preparation and teacher-facing review, but it is not used to judge pupil spelling scores.

Wordloom is intended to minimise pupil exposure to AI-generated content during live pupil test-taking.

2. Where AI may support teachers

AI may support teachers with preparation tasks, such as suggesting short sentences, word meanings, or spelling materials. These features are there to help teachers prepare resources more quickly while keeping the teacher in control of what is assigned.

Teachers can review or adjust context support in the test builder before assigning work.

3. What pupils do not do with AI

Wordloom does not provide pupils with open AI chat. Pupils do not type free-form questions to an AI assistant inside the pupil spelling test experience.

AI is not called while pupils are completing spelling tests.

4. Spelling tests and scoring

Pupil spelling scores are not judged by AI. Spelling test marking is based on the pupil's submitted answer against the expected spelling or task response.

Context support is separate from spelling scores. Optional help text can support understanding, but it does not change whether a submitted spelling answer is marked correct.

5. Context support

Context support may include short sentences or meanings that help pupils understand the word they are working with. AI-supported context is designed to avoid giving spelling clues, but this is not a guarantee that every possible clue can be prevented.

Baseline spelling evidence remains separate from optional meaning or context support.

6. Teacher analytics support

AI may support teacher-facing analytics summaries or questions. This support is designed to help teachers review patterns in spelling activity, such as accuracy, repeated attempts, or areas that may need follow-up.

AI analytics support should be treated as a planning aid. It does not replace teacher judgement, school safeguarding procedures, specialist assessment, or professional review.

7. Data and prompts

Teacher-facing AI prompts may include the words, instructions, or analytics context needed to produce the requested support. Staff should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into AI prompts.

Schools should use the minimum information needed when asking for AI-supported preparation or analytics help.

8. Limits of AI support

AI output can be incomplete, unsuitable, or need editing. Wordloom uses AI as support for teacher preparation and review, not as a replacement for teacher checking or school decision-making.

Teachers should review AI-supported wording before assigning work where it will be visible to pupils.

9. Future policy updates

Wordloom may update this statement as AI features, product controls, or school requirements change. Any future wording should continue to describe what the product actually enforces.

10. Contact

For questions about Wordloom AI use, schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.
