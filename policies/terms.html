Wordloom policy

Terms of Use

Last reviewed: May 2026

These terms explain how Wordloom is expected to be used by schools, staff and pupils for school-managed spelling and literacy activity. Separate written agreements may apply for school use, data processing, subscriptions, pilots or support arrangements.

1. Purpose of these terms

These terms explain practical expectations for using Wordloom. By using Wordloom, schools and users are expected to use the service carefully, where applicable, and in line with school-managed spelling and literacy activity.

This page is not a Data Processing Agreement. It should be read alongside the Privacy Notice, Safeguarding and Pupil Safety Statement, Security Statement, AI Use Statement, and any separate written arrangements that apply.

2. Who may use Wordloom

Wordloom is provided for schools and school-managed use. Schools are responsible for deciding whether Wordloom is suitable for their setting, pupils, devices, staff workflows and local policies.

Staff should use Wordloom only where they are authorised by their school. Pupils should use Wordloom only as directed by their school, teacher or other authorised staff member.

3. School responsibilities

Schools remain responsible for deciding how Wordloom fits within their teaching, safeguarding, data protection, accessibility, device management and supervision arrangements.

Schools are responsible for staff account management, pupil credentials, device and browser settings, local policies, safeguarding procedures, and appropriate supervision of pupil use.

4. Staff and pupil accounts

Staff should only use accounts, roles, permissions and school data they are authorised to access. Staff should not share sign-in access or use another person's account.

Pupil accounts and credentials are school-managed. Schools should decide how usernames, PINs or other pupil access details are issued, stored, reset and supervised in their setting.

5. Acceptable use

Users must not try to access another school's data, misuse accounts, bypass access controls, disrupt the service, reverse engineer the product, scrape content or data, or upload harmful content, code or files.

Users should not use Wordloom in a way that could harm pupils, staff, schools, Wordloom systems, or other users. Schools should contact Wordloom if they suspect unauthorised access, account misuse or product behaviour that may affect school data or pupil use.

6. Teaching content and pupil work

Wordloom supports teacher-managed spelling tests, assignments, pupil practice and progress review. Staff remain responsible for checking teaching content before assigning it to pupils.

Pupil work and spelling evidence should be used as part of wider teaching review. Wordloom may help organise spelling activity and evidence, but schools remain responsible for teaching decisions and pupil support.

7. AI-supported features

AI-supported features may help staff with preparation or teacher-facing analytics where those features are used. They are support tools and should not replace professional judgement, teacher review, safeguarding procedures or school decision-making.

Staff should review AI-supported wording before assigning work where it will be visible to pupils, and should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into prompts or free-text support requests.

8. Service changes and availability

Wordloom may change, improve, suspend or withdraw features as the product, school needs, suppliers, technical requirements or support arrangements change.

Schools should plan teaching and access arrangements with appropriate local alternatives where needed, especially where a device, browser, network setting, account issue or product change affects use.

9. Data protection and related policies

Wordloom publishes related policy pages that explain privacy, safeguarding, cookies and browser storage, accessibility, security and AI-supported use. Schools should review those pages alongside these terms.

Separate written agreements may apply for school use, data processing, subscriptions, pilots or support arrangements. Schools should contact Wordloom if they need to discuss the arrangements that apply to their use of the service.

10. What Wordloom does not replace

Wordloom does not replace teacher judgement, safeguarding systems, specialist assessment, accessibility assessment, device management, filtering and monitoring, parental communication, or school statutory responsibilities.

Schools remain responsible for deciding how Wordloom is used with pupils and for following their own procedures when concerns, access needs or support needs arise.

11. Contact

For questions about these terms, school use, support arrangements or related policy pages, schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.

For pupil safeguarding concerns, contact the school in the first instance and follow the school's safeguarding procedures.

12. Updates to these terms

Wordloom may update these terms as the product, school requirements, suppliers, support processes or related policy pages change.

Future wording should continue to describe practical expectations and avoid unsupported claims about legal completeness, availability, compliance or suitability for every school context.
