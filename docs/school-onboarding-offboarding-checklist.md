# Wordloom School Onboarding / Offboarding Checklist

Status: Internal draft for review  
Last reviewed: 5 May 2026

## 1. Purpose

This checklist helps Wordloom manage school setup, launch readiness, support ownership, offboarding, export/deletion requests and closure records.

It is intended to support school onboarding, DPO/procurement/security review and internal operational consistency. It should help make sure that school-specific decisions, evidence, support routes and known limitations are recorded before and during use of Wordloom.

This checklist supports operational readiness and school review. It does not replace school, DPO, legal or procurement review.

## 2. Status and limitations

- This is an internal working checklist, not a public policy page.
- This is not legal advice and is not a legal contract.
- This checklist does not replace review by the school, DPO, legal advisers, procurement teams or security reviewers.
- Some onboarding, export, deletion, support or offboarding actions may require manual support steps or supplier confirmation.
- Unknown or unverified details should be marked clearly as `To be confirmed`.
- This checklist should not be used to claim full GDPR compliance, fully automated onboarding/offboarding, or instant/complete data export or deletion.

## 8. Export/deletion/offboarding handling notes

- Export, deletion and offboarding may involve manual support steps.
- Supplier logs, backups and support records may have separate retention terms.
- Some records may need to be retained for legal, security, audit or operational reasons.
- School instructions should be authorised, recorded and checked against the agreed scope.
- The Wordloom retention/deletion/export procedure should be followed alongside this checklist.
- Do not claim instant deletion or complete automated export unless that capability has been separately built, tested and verified.
- Do not treat an offboarding request as complete until manual/support steps, supplier dependencies, evidence records and school communications have been reviewed.
- Any unknown or unverified handling details should be marked `To be confirmed`.

## 10. Risk notes

- Supplier evidence may be incomplete or may need further confirmation before school review.
- The authorised school contact route may be unclear, especially during staff turnover or trust-level procurement.
- Import files may contain more data than is needed for Wordloom use.
- Role or scope misconfiguration could expose data to the wrong staff group or school context.
- Offboarding, export or deletion may depend on manual support steps.
- Supplier backup, log and support-record retention may limit what can be deleted immediately.
- AI prompt or support-record handling may require further confirmation before making school-facing assurances.
- Accessibility limitations may not yet be fully tested across all relevant pupil, staff, device and assistive technology scenarios.
- Public claims must not become stronger than the operational evidence held internally.
- Any open compliance, procurement or security questions should remain marked as `To be confirmed` until reviewed.

## 12. Review cadence

This checklist should be reviewed:

- before each school onboarding;
- before first live rollout;
- after any onboarding/offboarding issue;
- after supplier or hosting changes;
- after AI/data-flow changes;
- after major role/access changes;
- after retention/export/deletion process changes;
- at least annually.

This checklist is an internal working document. It should be reviewed alongside Wordloom's data processing agreement draft, privacy notice, school data use statement, AI use statement, safeguarding statement, sub-processor information, supplier due diligence checklist, accessibility findings log, incident response process, and retention/deletion/export procedure. It supports operational readiness and school review, but does not replace school/DPO/legal/procurement review.
