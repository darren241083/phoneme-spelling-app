School Data Processing / Data Use Statement

Last reviewed: May 2026

This page is public transparency information and not the full legal Data Processing Agreement. It explains how Wordloom may use school-managed data where a school uses Wordloom for spelling and literacy activity.

1. Purpose of this statement

This statement is for schools, staff, pupils, parents and carers who want to understand how Wordloom may use school-managed data in and around the service.

It is intended to explain practical data use in clear terms. Separate written terms may apply for school use, pilots, subscriptions or support arrangements.

2. Controller and processor roles

Where a school uses Wordloom, the school is usually the controller for school-managed pupil data. The school decides how Wordloom is used with its pupils and is usually responsible for the lawful basis, notices to families, and pupil data requests.

Wordloom is likely to act as processor for school-managed pupil use, acting on the school's instructions. Product setup and customer arrangements may vary, so this statement should not be treated as legal certainty for every situation.

Wordloom may act as controller for enquiries, demos, support and business administration.

3. School-managed data Wordloom may process

Wordloom may process school details, staff account details, staff roles, permissions, access approvals, pupil account details, class, form or year membership, spelling tests and assignments.

Wordloom may also process pupil attempts, responses, scores, progress and analytics evidence, Spelling Bee results where used, context support such as sentence or meaning cache records, teacher analytics chat or support information where used, and support or contact details.

4. Why school data is used

School data may be used to provide the service, manage staff and pupil access, create and assign spelling work, record attempts, show progress, support teacher review, support school administration, support security, and respond to support requests.

Wordloom aims to use the minimum information needed for these purposes and to keep school-managed data within school-scoped product areas where the product supports that.

5. Pupil spelling evidence and analytics

Pupil spelling evidence may include assigned words, submitted responses, whether a response matched the expected spelling or task response, attempt counts, completion information, scores, Spelling Bee results where used, and progress summaries.

This evidence may help staff review spelling activity, repeated attempts, class or group patterns, and areas that may need follow-up. It should be treated as teaching evidence, not as a specialist assessment or diagnosis.

6. AI-supported features and school data

Pupils do not use open AI chat, and AI is not called while pupils complete spelling tests. AI may support teacher preparation and teacher-facing analytics where those features are used.

Teacher-facing analytics support may include analytics context sent to AI so the requested support can be generated. Staff should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into AI prompts and should use the minimum information needed.

7. Access controls and school scoping

Access is intended to be role-based and school-scoped where supported. Staff roles, permissions, active school context and access approvals may be used to limit access to the school data needed for a user's role.

School-managed pupil data should be treated as school-scoped. These controls are intended to reduce access outside the relevant school context, while recognising that no online service can remove every risk.

8. Support and operational access

Wordloom support or operational access should be limited to what is needed to provide, maintain, secure or support the service.

Support requests may require Wordloom to review relevant school, account, technical, usage or support information so that issues can be understood and resolved.

9. Retention, deletion and export

Retention, deletion and export may depend on school instructions, product setup, record type, operational needs, and separate written terms. Schools should contact Wordloom if they need to discuss retention, deletion or export for their use of the service.

For pupil data requests, pupils, parents and carers should usually contact the school in the first instance. The school is usually best placed to verify the request and decide how it should be handled.

10. School responsibilities

Schools remain responsible for the lawful basis for their use of Wordloom, notices to families, staff account management, pupil credentials, local devices, safeguarding, internal policies, and responding to pupil or parent requests.

Schools should decide whether Wordloom is suitable for their setting and how it fits with their teaching, data protection, safeguarding, accessibility, device management and supervision arrangements.

11. Relationship with other policy pages

This statement should be read alongside the Privacy Notice, Security Statement, Safeguarding and Pupil Safety Statement, AI Use Statement, Cookie Notice, Accessibility Statement and Terms of Use.

Those pages explain related product behaviour, shared responsibilities and limits. Separate written terms may apply for school use, pilots, subscriptions or support arrangements.

12. Contact

For questions about school data processing, data use, support arrangements or related policy pages, schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.

For pupil data requests connected to school-managed use, contact the school in the first instance.

13. Updates to this statement

Wordloom may update this statement as the product, school requirements, suppliers, data use, support processes or related policy pages change.

Future wording should continue to describe practical data use and avoid unsupported claims about legal completeness, compliance, hosting, retention, deletion or suitability for every school context.
