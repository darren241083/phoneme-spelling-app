Wordloom policy

DPIA Support

Last reviewed: May 2026

This page supports schools with their own Data Protection Impact Assessment process for Wordloom. It is not the school's finished DPIA record and is not legal advice or data protection advice.

1. Purpose of this DPIA support page

This page brings together product information that may help schools consider data protection risks, controls and local responsibilities when assessing Wordloom.

Schools remain responsible for deciding whether a DPIA is required, completing it, approving it through their own governance, and reviewing it with the school or trust DPO where appropriate.

2. What Wordloom is used for

Wordloom is a school-facing spelling and literacy app. It helps staff create spelling tests, assign pupil follow-up practice, review spelling evidence, and understand progress patterns.

Pupil accounts are school-managed and are intended for assigned school spelling activity, not independent public account creation by children.

3. Controller and processor roles

The school is usually the controller for school-managed pupil data because the school decides how Wordloom is used with its pupils.

Wordloom is likely to act as processor for school-managed use, acting on the school's instructions. Product setup and written arrangements may vary, so schools should consider the role position that applies to their own setup.

Wordloom may act as controller for enquiries, demos, support and business administration.

4. Data likely involved

Data likely involved may include school details, staff names, staff email addresses, roles, permissions, access approvals, pupil account details, class or form membership, and school-managed pupil identifiers.

Where used, Wordloom may also process spelling tests, assignments, submitted responses, scores, attempt counts, completion information, progress summaries, Spelling Bee results, context support, teacher analytics chat records, support details, and browser or session storage needed for product operation.

5. Why the data is processed

Data may be processed to provide the service, manage staff and pupil access, create and assign spelling work, record attempts, show progress, support teacher review, support school administration, support security, and respond to support requests.

For pupil data requests, pupils, parents and carers should usually contact the school in the first instance. The school is usually best placed to verify the request and decide how it should be handled.

6. AI use and pupil safety controls

Pupils do not use open AI chat in Wordloom, and AI is not called while pupils complete spelling tests.

AI may support teacher preparation and teacher-facing analytics where those features are used. Staff should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into AI prompts and should use the minimum information needed.

Teachers should review AI-supported wording before assigning work where it will be visible to pupils.

7. Access, security and school scoping

Wordloom is intended to use school scoping and role-based access where supported. Staff roles, permissions, active school context and access approvals may help limit access to school data needed for a user's role.

Teacher sign-in may use Supabase Auth and Google OAuth. Browser or session storage may support sign-in, school context, accessibility preferences and product operation.

Schools should consider their own staff account management, local devices, browser access, network controls, leaver processes, and security reporting arrangements.

8. Possible risks for schools to consider

Schools should consider risks linked to children's data, school-managed pupil identifiers, spelling evidence, progress analytics, staff access and permissions, AI-supported teacher analytics or preparation, browser and session storage, supplier or sub-processor reliance, accessibility and inclusion, safeguarding and pupil safety, and retention, deletion or export arrangements.

Schools should also consider local rollout, staff training, account management, device supervision, and how Wordloom fits with existing data protection and safeguarding procedures.

9. Risk-reduction measures built into Wordloom

Risk-reduction measures may include school-managed pupil accounts, teacher-managed assignments, no pupil-to-pupil messaging, no public posting, no open pupil AI chat, teacher review of pupil-facing content, and separation of optional context support from spelling scores.

Other measures may include school scoping, role-based access where supported, staff access approvals, server-side helpers for some privileged actions, and policy pages explaining privacy, security, safeguarding, cookies, accessibility, AI use, school data use and sub-processors.

These measures may reduce risk, but they do not remove every risk or replace school review.

10. Areas schools should assess locally

Schools should assess lawful basis, privacy notices to families, age and vulnerability considerations, role allocation, staff training, pupil credential handling, device and browser management, filtering and monitoring, accessibility needs, retention expectations, data export or deletion needs, and incident response routes.

Schools should also assess which suppliers or sub-processors are relevant to their setup and whether local procurement, information governance, DPO or trust review is needed.

11. Suggested DPIA questions

Schools may wish to consider what pupil and staff data will be added, who will manage accounts, which staff roles need access, how pupil credentials will be issued, whether AI-supported features will be used, and what staff guidance is needed.

Schools may also wish to consider how long data should be kept, how deletion or export requests will be handled, how accessibility needs will be checked, how safeguarding concerns will be escalated, and how supplier information will be reviewed.

12. Related policy pages

Schools should read this page alongside the Privacy Notice, School Data Use Statement, Security Statement, Safeguarding and Pupil Safety Statement, AI Use Statement, Cookie Notice, Accessibility Statement, Terms of Use, and Sub-processors page.

Those pages explain related product behaviour, shared responsibilities and known limits.

13. Contact

Schools and DPOs can contact Wordloom with questions about school data processing, suppliers, product controls, or information needed for local assessment.

For questions about this page, schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.

14. Updates to this page

Wordloom may update this page as the product, suppliers, data use, school requirements, support processes, or related policy pages change.

Future wording should continue to support school review without replacing the school's own DPIA process, governance, legal advice, data protection advice, or DPO review.
