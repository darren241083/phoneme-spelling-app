Safeguarding & Pupil Safety Statement

Last reviewed: May 2026

Wordloom is designed to reduce pupil-facing online safety risks in a school-controlled spelling app. This statement explains the product design choices that support safer school use, and the responsibilities that remain with schools.

1. Purpose of this statement

This statement is for schools, staff, parents and carers who want to understand how Wordloom is intended to support safer pupil-facing spelling activity.

It describes product safeguards and limits. It does not replace school safeguarding systems, statutory responsibilities, or decisions made by a school's designated safeguarding lead or safeguarding team.

2. Pupil-facing safety design

Wordloom is not a social network. It is built around school-controlled spelling activities that are prepared, assigned and reviewed by staff.

Pupils do not have pupil-to-pupil messaging, public posting, open web search, or open AI chat inside Wordloom. Pupil work is teacher-managed through assigned spelling activities.

3. AI and pupil safety

Wordloom does not provide pupils with open AI chat. AI is not called while pupils complete spelling tests.

AI support may be used in teacher-facing preparation or analytics features. These features are intended to support staff review and planning, not to replace teacher judgement or school decision-making.

4. Communication and sharing

Wordloom does not provide pupil-to-pupil messaging or public posting. The pupil experience is focused on spelling activities assigned by the school.

Schools remain responsible for wider communication controls, device settings, filtering and monitoring arrangements, and supervision outside the Wordloom product.

5. Teacher and school control

Wordloom is intended to support teacher-managed spelling work. Staff create, assign and review spelling tests and follow-up practice for pupils in their school context.

Teachers can review or adjust context support in the test builder before assigning work where those features are used.

6. Staff access and school scoping

Staff access is designed to be role-based and school-scoped where the product supports this. These controls are intended to reduce access to information or activity that is not needed for a user's role or school context.

Schools should manage staff accounts, permissions and internal access expectations in line with their own safeguarding, data protection and account management procedures.

7. Content and spelling context support

Wordloom may support spelling context such as short sentences or meanings. AI-supported context is designed to avoid giving spelling clues, but this is not a guarantee that every possible clue can be prevented.

Staff should review pupil-facing wording before assigning work, especially where context support has been suggested or edited.

8. What Wordloom does not replace

Wordloom does not replace a school's safeguarding policy, designated safeguarding lead processes, filtering and monitoring duties, device management, parental communication, incident response, or statutory safeguarding obligations.

Schools remain responsible for safeguarding decisions and for deciding how Wordloom fits within their wider online safety, curriculum, pastoral and technical arrangements.

9. Reporting concerns

If staff see a safeguarding or pupil welfare concern, they should follow their school's safeguarding procedures and report to the appropriate school safeguarding lead.

Schools can contact Wordloom about product, privacy, security or support concerns connected to the service, but Wordloom should not be treated as the school's safeguarding reporting route.

10. Updates to this statement

Wordloom may update this statement as product features, school requirements or safety controls change. Future wording should continue to describe what the product actually supports and avoid unsupported safety claims.

11. Contact

For questions about this statement, schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.

For pupil safeguarding concerns, contact the school in the first instance and follow the school's safeguarding procedures.
