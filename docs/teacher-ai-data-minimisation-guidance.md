# Teacher AI Data Minimisation Guidance

**Status:** Internal/staff-facing draft for review  
**Last reviewed:** 5 May 2026

## 1. Purpose

This guidance helps staff use Wordloom teacher-facing AI support carefully.

It supports safer prompting, data minimisation, staff review and school confidence when using AI-assisted support for teaching preparation, spelling words, sentences, context support, analytics summaries and related teacher workflows.

This guidance is not legal advice, is not a full AI policy and is not a substitute for school safeguarding policy, school AI policy, data protection guidance, DPO advice or professional judgement.

## 2. Core Principle: Minimum Necessary Information

Staff should only include the minimum necessary information needed for the teaching or support task.

Where possible, use generalised context rather than identifiable pupil details. Many useful prompts can be written without pupil names, individual histories, safeguarding context, health information, family circumstances or other sensitive details.

Good prompts usually describe the learning task, year group, reading level, spelling pattern, target sound, word list or teaching goal without identifying an individual pupil.

## 3. What Not To Include In Prompts

Where a school has a specific approved process for certain information, staff should follow school/DPO/safeguarding guidance. If unsure, pause and ask for advice before entering the information.

| Avoid entering | Why this matters | Safer approach |
|---|---|---|
| Pupil full names | Names can make a prompt identifiable when the task often does not need this. | Use "a Year 7 pupil", "a small group" or "the class" where appropriate. |
| Personal identifiers | Identifiers such as email addresses, usernames, IDs or dates of birth can increase privacy risk. | Remove identifiers and describe the learning need in general terms. |
| Safeguarding details | Safeguarding information is sensitive and may need specialist handling. | Do not include safeguarding details in prompts; follow school safeguarding routes. |
| Health or medical information | Health information may be sensitive and is rarely needed for spelling support. | Describe only the teaching need, such as "needs shorter instructions", if school guidance allows. |
| Family circumstances | Family details can be sensitive and usually are not needed for the learning task. | Use neutral teaching context without family information. |
| Behaviour incidents | Behaviour details can be sensitive, subjective and unnecessary for prompt quality. | Ask for a neutral activity, sentence or explanation without behaviour history. |
| SEN details or diagnostic labels | SEN information and diagnostic labels may be sensitive and should not be added unless specifically approved. | Describe the practical teaching support needed in general terms, following school guidance. |
| Disability/access needs | Access needs may be sensitive and should be handled carefully. | Use general accessibility needs only where necessary, such as "use plain language" or "provide step-by-step instructions". |
| Attendance concerns | Attendance information is usually not needed for AI-generated teaching support. | Ask for a catch-up activity without explaining attendance history. |
| Child protection concerns | Child protection information needs safeguarding handling and should not be entered into prompts. | Stop and follow school safeguarding procedures. |
| Free-text comments copied from school systems | Comments may contain names, sensitive context or third-party information. | Summarise the teaching need without copying identifiable or sensitive text. |
| Screenshots containing identifiable pupil information | Screenshots may include names, scores, notes, class lists or other personal data. | Avoid uploading or sharing screenshots with identifiable information; use a general written prompt instead. |
| Any information not needed for the learning task | Extra context can create unnecessary data risk and may distract from the teaching goal. | Keep the prompt focused on the word, sound, spelling pattern, activity or explanation needed. |

## 4. Safer Prompt Patterns

Unsafe / avoid:

- "Write feedback for Emily Jones, who has struggled with spelling since last term..."
- "This pupil has ADHD and behaviour issues. Make a task for them."
- "Use this screenshot of my class analytics with pupil names to identify who needs help."

Safer / preferred:

- "Create a short Year 7-friendly sentence using the word 'because'."
- "Suggest three neutral practice sentences for pupils working on the /sh/ sound."
- "Explain this spelling pattern in simple language for a lower-attaining KS3 pupil."
- "Create a generic teacher note about common spelling errors in this word list."
- "Suggest a support activity for a small group struggling with split digraphs, without referring to individual pupils."
- "Summarise common next-step teaching points for a class practising these spellings, without naming pupils."
- "Create context support for the word 'island' without giving away the spelling answer."

## 5. Teacher Review Before Pupil Use

AI suggestions are drafts and support only. They are not automatically safe, correct, suitable or approved.

Before using AI output with pupils, staff should check:

- Accuracy.
- Age suitability.
- Tone.
- Reading level.
- Cultural sensitivity.
- Safeguarding appropriateness.
- Whether the sentence accidentally gives away the spelling answer.
- Whether context support remains separate from spelling scoring.
- Whether the output fits school expectations.

Staff remain responsible for reviewing and adjusting outputs before use with pupils.

## 6. Analytics Assistant And Pupil-Related Summaries

Teacher-facing analytics support may involve class or pupil-related summaries where enabled. Staff should avoid adding unnecessary identifiable details in follow-up prompts.

Where possible, ask general questions, such as:

- "What common spelling patterns should I revisit with this group?"
- "Suggest neutral next-step teaching ideas for pupils struggling with these words."
- "How could I explain this trend to a teacher without naming pupils?"

Exports, screenshots and copied data should be handled carefully. Staff should avoid entering identifiable pupil information, sensitive details or copied records unless there is a specific approved school process and the minimum necessary information is being used.

Concerns should be escalated through the agreed school or Wordloom support route.

## 7. When To Pause And Ask For Advice

Pause and seek advice when:

- Safeguarding or child protection information would be needed.
- A prompt would include health, family, behaviour, SEN or sensitive personal details.
- A pupil or parent/carer has raised a concern.
- An AI output seems unsuitable, biased, unsafe or inaccurate.
- Staff are unsure whether information is necessary.
- Data from another system would need to be copied into a prompt.
- An incident or suspected data issue has occurred.

Depending on the concern, advice may be needed from the school safeguarding lead, AI lead, DPO/data protection lead, senior staff or Wordloom support.

## 8. Quick Checklist For Staff

| Before using AI support | Check |
|---|---|
| Is the prompt needed for a teaching/support purpose? | To be checked |
| Can I do this without pupil names or identifiers? | To be checked |
| Have I removed unnecessary sensitive information? | To be checked |
| Am I using the minimum necessary context? | To be checked |
| Will I review the output before pupil use? | To be checked |
| Do I know who to ask if unsure? | To be checked |

## 9. If Something Goes Wrong

If an AI output or prompt raises a concern:

- Stop using or sharing the output.
- Record what happened.
- Do not delete evidence if it may relate to an incident.
- Report through the agreed school/Wordloom support route.
- Escalate safeguarding, data protection or AI concerns where appropriate.
- Link the concern to the incident response process and AI risk assessment notes where needed.

## 10. Review Cadence

Review this guidance:

- Before school rollout.
- When AI features change.
- When school AI/data protection guidance changes.
- After an AI-related concern.
- After supplier/provider changes.
- At least annually.

## 11. Internal Caveat

This guidance supports careful staff use of teacher-facing AI features in Wordloom. It should be reviewed alongside the school's AI policy, safeguarding policy, data protection guidance, DPO advice, Wordloom's AI Use Statement, AI risk assessment notes, incident response process and data subject request handling checklist. It does not replace school policy, professional judgement or legal/DPO review.
