Security Statement

Last reviewed: May 2026

Wordloom is designed with school-controlled access and data separation in mind. This statement explains security-related product controls, shared responsibilities, known limits, and how schools can report concerns.

1. Purpose of this statement

This statement is for schools, staff, pupils, parents and carers who want to understand Wordloom's current security approach.

It is not a certification or formal audit. It describes controls that are present in the product where supported, plus responsibilities that remain with schools.

2. Access control

Staff access is intended to be role-based where the product supports this. Staff roles, permissions and approvals may be used to limit access to product areas and school information that are needed for a user's role.

Schools should manage staff accounts, access changes and leaver processes in line with their own internal policies.

3. School scoping and tenant separation

School data is intended to be school-scoped. The app includes active school context, school and tenant scoping, row-level security policies, staff access approvals, and audit or history records where those features are present.

These layered controls are designed to reduce access outside the relevant school context, while recognising that no online service can remove every risk.

4. Authentication

Teacher sign-in may use Supabase Auth and Google OAuth. Authentication sessions may be stored in browser storage so users can remain signed in and the app can handle sign-in redirects and session refresh.

Server-side helpers and functions are used for some privileged operations. These helpers can check teacher authentication and school access before carrying out supported actions.

5. Pupil accounts

Pupil access uses school-managed pupil credentials and server-side authentication or validation. Pupil accounts are intended for assigned spelling activity rather than independent account creation by pupils.

Schools remain responsible for managing how pupil credentials are issued, stored, reset and supervised in their own setting.

6. AI and pupil-facing safety controls

Pupils do not have open AI chat in Wordloom. AI is not called while pupils complete spelling tests.

AI-supported features are intended to support teacher preparation or teacher-facing analytics where used. Staff should avoid entering unnecessary pupil personal data into prompts or free-text support requests.

7. Operational access and support

Operational or support access should be limited to what is needed to provide, maintain, secure or support the service.

Support requests may require Wordloom to review relevant account, school, technical or usage information so that issues can be understood and resolved.

8. Security responsibilities shared with schools

Security is a shared responsibility. Schools remain responsible for staff accounts, local devices, browser access, network controls, internal policies, safeguarding procedures, and reporting concerns promptly.

Schools should use appropriate device management, account review, password or sign-in controls, and staff training for their local environment.

9. Known limitations

No online service can remove every risk. This page does not claim that Wordloom has completed a formal security audit, holds a security certification, or meets a particular security standard.

Schools should not rely on this statement as a complete technical security assessment. Product controls, hosting arrangements, suppliers and school requirements may change over time.

10. Reporting security concerns

Schools can contact Wordloom about security concerns, suspected unauthorised access, account issues, or product behaviour that may affect school data or pupil use.

When reporting a concern, schools should include the affected school, user or workflow, what happened, when it happened, and any steps already taken. Schools can contact hello@wordloom.co.uk.

11. Updates to this statement

Wordloom may update this statement as product controls, suppliers, security practices, school requirements or support processes change.

Future wording should continue to describe what the product actually supports and avoid unsupported security, certification or compliance claims.
